How the DHAP Process Works

A transparent, family-focused approach to foreclosure prevention

1

Apply & Assess

Submit your application and we'll evaluate your property, financial situation, and family needs within 5-7 days.

2

Purchase & Relief

We purchase your property, pay off all debts, and provide immediate cash assistance for your family's needs.

3

Rental Period

Live in your home as our tenant for 12+ months at reduced rent while building credits toward buyback.

4

Buy Back Option

When ready, repurchase your home using accumulated rent credits and improved financial position.

Do You Qualify for DHAP?

Review these criteria to see if our program can help your family

Family Requirements

  • Primary residence for 12+ months
  • Genuine financial hardship situation
  • 60+ days mortgage delinquency
  • Minimum 15% equity in property
  • Desire to remain in the community
  • Willingness to participate in financial counseling

Property Requirements

  • Property value: $75,000 - $300,000
  • Structurally sound condition
  • Located within Houston area boundaries
  • Strong rental market potential
  • No history of mortgage fraud
  • Clear title and ownership

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our DHAP program

With DHAP, you get to stay in your home during the rental period and have the option to buy it back. Traditional sales mean you lose your home permanently and must find new housing immediately. We also provide relocation assistance and below-market rent to help you recover financially.
If you choose not to or cannot exercise the buyback option after 12 months, we'll provide 60 days' notice and assistance finding alternative housing. You'll have received 12+ months of stable housing at below-market rent, plus any accumulated rent credits can help with your transition.
DHAP rent is typically 70-80% of market rate. For example, if market rent is $1,200/month, you'd pay $840-$960. Plus, 25-30% of your rent payment becomes credits toward buying back your home - building equity while you rent.
Yes! Poor credit due to financial hardship doesn't disqualify you from DHAP. We focus on your situation, not your credit score. During the rental period, successful payments can actually help rebuild your credit for the eventual buyback.
