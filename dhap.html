The Challenge We Address

Families across Houston face financial hardship that threatens their stability and community integrity.

Foreclosure Crisis

Many homeowners face foreclosure due to temporary financial hardship, job loss, medical emergencies, or other life circumstances beyond their control.

  • • Families lose their homes and equity
  • • Credit damaged for years to come
  • • Children forced to change schools
  • • Community stability disrupted

Traditional Solutions Fall Short

Conventional approaches often don't provide the comprehensive support families need to rebuild financial stability.

  • • Bank modifications often insufficient
  • • Quick sale options below market value
  • • Limited time to find alternatives
  • • No pathway to rebuild equity

Community Impact

Foreclosures don't just affect individual families—they impact entire neighborhoods and communities.

  • • Vacant properties reduce property values
  • • Neighborhood deterioration accelerates
  • • Local schools lose enrollment
  • • Community cohesion breaks down

How the DHAP Program Works

A structured 4-step process designed to move quickly while ensuring all parties benefit.

1

Initial Assessment & Application

Homeowner submits application with property and financial information. Our team conducts initial evaluation within 48 hours to determine program eligibility and potential solutions.

  • • Complete application review
  • • Property valuation assessment
  • • Financial situation analysis
  • • Preliminary solution design
2

Comprehensive Evaluation

Detailed property inspection, market analysis, and financial structuring to create a customized solution that works for the family's specific situation and timeline.

  • • Professional property inspection
  • • Market comparative analysis
  • • Legal and title review
  • • Custom solution development
3

Agreement & Transition

If approved, we structure the purchase agreement and lease terms that allow the family to remain in their home while transferring ownership to our investment group.

  • • Purchase agreement execution
  • • Lease agreement preparation
  • • Closing coordination
  • • Transition support services
4

Ongoing Support & Management

Comprehensive support including property management, financial education, and potential pathway for family to repurchase the property when their situation improves.

  • • Professional property management
  • • Financial education programs
  • • Regular check-ins and support
  • • Repurchase option development
Typical Timeline: 30-45 Days from Application to Closing

Program Qualification Criteria

DHAP is designed to help families in genuine financial distress while ensuring investment viability.

Homeowner Requirements

  • ✓ Genuine Financial Hardship
    Documented financial distress due to job loss, medical issues, divorce, or other qualifying circumstances
  • ✓ Minimum Equity Position
    At least 15% equity in the property to ensure mutually beneficial transaction structure
  • ✓ Commitment to Program
    Willingness to participate in financial education and maintain property as responsible tenant
  • ✓ Houston Area Location
    Property located within The Houston 100's target investment areas

Property Requirements

  • ✓ Value Range
    Property value between $75,000 - $300,000 to align with program investment criteria
  • ✓ Condition Standards
    Property in reasonable condition with manageable repair requirements
  • ✓ Clear Title
    Clear title with no major legal encumbrances or complications
  • ✓ Rental Viability
    Property suitable for rental income generation in stable neighborhood
