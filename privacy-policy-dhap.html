Distressed Homeowner Assistance Program

Last Updated: January 31, 2025
Effective Date: January 31, 2025

Privacy Commitment

Houston 100 Investment Group LLC is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information with care and respect. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information in connection with our Distressed Homeowner Assistance Program (DHAP).

1. Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to evaluate your eligibility for the DHAP program and to provide our services effectively. The information collected includes:

1.1 Personal Identification Information

  • Full name and contact information (address, phone, email)
  • Social Security numbers for all adult household members
  • Date of birth and government-issued identification
  • Employment history and current employment status
  • Marital status and family composition

1.2 Financial Information

  • Income statements, tax returns, and pay stubs
  • Bank statements and account information
  • Credit reports and credit scores
  • Mortgage statements and payment history
  • Debt obligations and monthly expenses
  • Details of financial hardship circumstances

1.3 Property Information

  • Property address and legal description
  • Property value estimates and appraisals
  • Mortgage and lien information
  • Property condition and inspection reports
  • Insurance information and claims history
  • Property tax records and payment status

1.4 Program-Related Information

  • Application responses and supporting documentation
  • Communication records (emails, calls, meetings)
  • Program participation history and outcomes
  • Payment records and rent credit accumulation
  • Property maintenance and inspection records

2. How We Collect Information

2.1 Direct Collection

Most information is collected directly from you through:

  • Online application forms on our website
  • Detailed application documents and supporting materials
  • Phone conversations and in-person meetings
  • Email communications and document submissions

2.2 Third-Party Sources

With your consent, we may also collect information from:

  • Credit reporting agencies
  • Mortgage lenders and servicers
  • Property appraisers and inspectors
  • Government records (tax, property, court)
  • Employers and financial institutions (with authorization)

2.3 Technology and Website Collection

Our website may collect:

  • IP addresses and browser information
  • Website usage data and page views
  • Form submission data through secure channels
  • Cookies for website functionality (non-tracking)

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Program Evaluation and Administration

  • Assess eligibility for the DHAP program
  • Evaluate property value and investment potential
  • Process applications and make program decisions
  • Structure purchase and rental agreements
  • Monitor program compliance and performance

3.2 Communication and Support

  • Respond to inquiries and provide program updates
  • Coordinate property evaluations and transactions
  • Provide financial counseling and education resources
  • Send important notices and program communications

3.3 Legal and Compliance

  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Protect against fraud and verify information accuracy
  • Maintain records for audit and reporting purposes
  • Enforce program terms and rental agreements

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information in the following circumstances:

4.1 Service Providers

We may share information with trusted service providers who assist with:

  • Property appraisals and inspections
  • Legal services and title work
  • Financial counseling and credit services
  • Property management and maintenance
  • Technology services (form processing, data storage)

All service providers are bound by confidentiality agreements and may only use information for the specified purposes.

4.2 Business Partners

Limited information may be shared with:

  • Houston 100 investors and members (aggregated, non-identifying data only)
  • Community partners providing support services
  • Financial institutions for loan processing or verification

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by:

  • Court orders, subpoenas, or legal process
  • Government investigations or regulatory requests
  • Emergency situations involving safety or security
  • Protection of Houston 100's legal rights and interests

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 Form Processing (Formspree)

Our website uses Formspree to process application forms. Information submitted through forms is transmitted securely to Formspree and then to our systems. Formspree's privacy policy governs their handling of this data during transmission.

5.2 Data Management (Airtable)

Application and program data is stored and managed using Airtable's secure platform. Airtable maintains enterprise-level security standards and data protection measures.

5.3 Communication Services

We may use third-party services for email communication, document sharing, and video conferencing. These services are selected based on their security standards and privacy protections.

Data Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: Sensitive data is encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Access Controls: Limited access on a need-to-know basis
  • Secure Storage: Information stored on secure, professionally managed platforms
  • Regular Updates: Security systems and software are regularly updated
  • Employee Training: Staff trained on privacy and security best practices
  • Incident Response: Procedures in place for addressing security incidents

6. Data Retention

We retain your information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy:

Information Type Retention Period Purpose
Application Data 7 years after decision Legal compliance, audit
Program Participation Records 7 years after program completion Tax, legal, performance tracking
Financial Information 7 years after last transaction Tax reporting, legal compliance
Communication Records 3 years after last contact Service improvement, dispute resolution
Website Usage Data 2 years Analytics, service improvement

Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: Request copies of your personal information we hold
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Portability: Request transfer of your information in a portable format
  • Objection: Object to certain uses of your information
  • Withdrawal: Withdraw consent for processing (where applicable)

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided below.

7. Children's Privacy

The DHAP program is designed for adults facing foreclosure. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

Information about minor children in the household may be collected as part of family composition and program planning, but only with appropriate parental consent.

8. California Privacy Rights

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected and how it's used
  • Right to delete personal information (subject to exceptions)
  • Right to opt-out of sale of personal information (we do not sell information)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising CCPA rights

To exercise these rights, California residents may contact us using the information below.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. When we make changes:

  • We will post the updated policy on our website
  • We will update the "Last Updated" date
  • For material changes, we will notify active program participants via email
  • Continued participation in the program constitutes acceptance of the updated policy

Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you promptly in accordance with applicable law. Notifications will include information about the nature of the breach, the information involved, and steps we are taking to address the situation.

10. International Data Transfers

Your information is primarily processed and stored within the United States. If information is transferred internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your privacy rights.

11. Automated Decision Making

We may use automated tools to help evaluate applications and assess risk, but significant decisions about program participation are made by human reviewers. You have the right to request human review of any automated decision that affects you.
