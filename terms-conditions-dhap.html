Distressed Homeowner Assistance Program

Last Updated: January 31, 2025

These Terms and Conditions govern your participation in Houston 100 Investment Group LLC's Distressed Homeowner Assistance Program (DHAP). By submitting an application or participating in the program, you agree to be bound by these terms. Please read carefully and consult with legal counsel if you have questions.

1. Program Overview

The Distressed Homeowner Assistance Program (DHAP) is offered by Houston 100 Investment Group LLC ("Houston 100", "we", "us", or "our") to help qualifying families avoid foreclosure while maintaining housing stability. The program involves the purchase of distressed properties with an option for the original homeowner to repurchase the property under specific terms and conditions.

1.1 Program Structure

DHAP operates under a "Purchase with Buyback Option" model where:

  • Houston 100 purchases the property from the distressed homeowner
  • The homeowner becomes a tenant for a minimum 12-month period
  • Rent payments include credits toward potential buyback
  • The homeowner has an option to repurchase the property under agreed terms

2. Eligibility Requirements

2.1 Homeowner Qualifications

To be eligible for DHAP, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Property must be primary residence for at least 12 months
  • Demonstrate genuine financial hardship (job loss, medical emergency, etc.)
  • Be at least 60 days delinquent on mortgage payments
  • Have minimum 15% equity in the property
  • Desire to remain in the community
  • Agree to participate in financial counseling and education programs
  • No history of mortgage fraud or intentional default

2.2 Property Qualifications

  • Property value between $75,000 and $300,000
  • Structurally sound condition suitable for rental
  • Located within Houston 100's designated service area
  • Strong rental market demand
  • Clear title with no disputed ownership
  • Compliance with local housing codes and regulations

3. Application Process

3.1 Initial Application

Applications may be submitted through our website form or detailed application process. All information provided must be accurate and complete. False or misleading information may result in immediate disqualification.

3.2 Evaluation Timeline

  • Initial review: 24-48 hours
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 5-7 business days
  • Ethics Committee review: 3-5 business days
  • Final decision notification: Within 10 business days of complete application

3.3 No Guarantee of Acceptance

Submission of an application does not guarantee acceptance into the DHAP program. Houston 100 reserves the right to decline any application for any reason consistent with applicable law.

4. Property Purchase Terms

4.1 Purchase Price

Houston 100 will purchase properties at 75-85% of current fair market value as determined by professional appraisal. The purchase price will be sufficient to pay off existing mortgages, liens, and provide relocation assistance to the homeowner.

4.2 Relocation Assistance

Qualifying families will receive $3,000-$8,000 in relocation assistance based on family size, circumstances, and need. This assistance is provided to help with transition costs and family stabilization.

4.3 Closing Process

All property purchases will be conducted through licensed real estate attorneys with proper title searches, inspections, and legal documentation. Closing typically occurs within 2-3 weeks of final approval.

5. Rental Period Terms

5.1 Rental Rate

During the rental period, tenants will pay 70-80% of fair market rental value. Rent is due on the first of each month and is considered late after the 5th day of the month.

5.2 Rent Credits

25-30% of each rent payment will be credited toward the buyback option. These credits accumulate monthly and can only be applied toward repurchasing the property.

5.3 Lease Terms

The initial lease term is 12 months with automatic month-to-month renewal unless either party provides 60 days written notice. Standard landlord-tenant laws apply to the rental relationship.

5.4 Property Maintenance

Tenants are responsible for:

  • Routine maintenance and upkeep
  • Minor repairs under $100
  • Utilities and services
  • Maintaining property in good condition

Houston 100 is responsible for major repairs, structural issues, and capital improvements.

6. Buyback Option Terms

6.1 Buyback Formula

The buyback price is calculated as: Current Fair Market Value + Houston 100's Total Investment + 10% Annual Return - Accumulated Rent Credits

6.2 Qualification for Buyback

To exercise the buyback option, tenants must:

  • Maintain 95% or better on-time payment record
  • Keep property in good condition per inspection
  • Complete financial education program
  • Demonstrate stable income and improved credit
  • Secure financing or provide adequate down payment

6.3 Buyback Timeline

The buyback option may be exercised any time after 12 months of successful tenancy. Houston 100 may extend the option period for qualifying families experiencing temporary setbacks.

6.4 Financing Options

Houston 100 may provide seller financing at competitive market rates or assist in connecting families with traditional lenders.

Important: Property Sale if Buyback Not Exercised

If the buyback option is not exercised or the tenant does not qualify, Houston 100 will sell the property on the open market. Tenants will receive 60 days written notice and assistance in finding alternative housing when possible.

7. Payment Failure and Remedies

7.1 Late Payment Policy

Rent payments more than 5 days late will incur a $50 late fee. Payments more than 15 days late will initiate our crisis intervention protocol.

7.2 Crisis Intervention

Houston 100 will work with families experiencing payment difficulties through:

  • Payment plan arrangements
  • Temporary rent reductions
  • Emergency assistance fund access
  • Financial counseling and support services

7.3 Eviction as Last Resort

Eviction will only be pursued after all reasonable assistance measures have been exhausted and in accordance with Texas landlord-tenant law.

8. Program Termination

8.1 Voluntary Termination

Families may terminate their participation in DHAP with 60 days written notice. Any accumulated rent credits will be forfeited upon voluntary termination before buyback.

8.2 Involuntary Termination

Houston 100 may terminate participation for:

  • Material breach of lease terms
  • Failure to maintain property condition
  • Chronic payment delinquency
  • Violation of program requirements

9. Legal Disclaimers and Limitations

9.1 No Guarantee of Success

Houston 100 makes no guarantee that participants will successfully repurchase their property or achieve specific financial outcomes. Success depends on individual circumstances and effort.

9.2 Market Risk

Property values may fluctuate during the program period. Buyback prices are subject to current market valuations and may be higher or lower than the original purchase price.

9.3 Legal Counsel Recommended

Participants are strongly encouraged to consult with independent legal counsel before entering into any agreements related to the DHAP program.

9.4 Limitation of Liability

Houston 100's liability is limited to direct damages and shall not exceed the amount of rent credits accumulated by the participant.

10. Dispute Resolution

10.1 Mediation First

Any disputes arising under this program will first be submitted to mediation through a mutually agreed mediator.

10.2 Arbitration

If mediation fails, disputes will be resolved through binding arbitration in Harris County, Texas, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.3 Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

Personal and financial information collected during the DHAP application and participation process is governed by our Privacy Policy. We are committed to protecting participant privacy and using information only for program-related purposes.
